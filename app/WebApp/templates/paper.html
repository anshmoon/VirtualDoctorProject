Abstract

One of the most important issues facing the globe today is heart disease. Prediction of cardiovascular disease is crucial. Hybrid machine learning (ML) has shown to be an excellent tool for making predictions and judgements based on the vast amounts of data generated by the healthcare sector and hospitals. Additionally, we have observed the employment of ML approaches in recent advancements across several IoT domains (IoT). Only a few research have used machine learning to predict cardiac disease. The primary focus of the research paper is on which patients, given certain medical characteristics, are more likely to suffer heart disease. To determine whether the patient is likely to receive a heart disease diagnosis or not, we developed a heart disease prediction algorithm using the patient's medical history. To forecast and categorise the patient with heart disease, we employed a variety of machine learning methods, including KNN , RANDOM FOREST, NAIVE BAYES  LOGISTIC REGRESSION and DECISION TREE .The narrative approach we suggest in this study tries to identify relevant features by utilising machine learning techniques, which improves the accuracy of cardiovascular disease prediction .The proposed prediction model includes combinations of many classification algorithms and characteristics. We create an improved efficiency with a 90% accuracy rate for the heart disease prediction model.

Keywords: Supervised learning algorithm, Random forest ,Naïve Bayes , Logistic regression , Decision tree , Heart diseases

Features that are positively correlated with the target features

Chest pain type:

 

Chest pain can be classified into several types based on its characteristics and underlying causes.

 

·        Angina, this is chest pain or discomfort caused by reduced blood flow to the heart. It usually feels like a pressure or tightness in the chest, and may also be felt in the arms, shoulders, neck, jaw, or back.

 

·        Atypical angina is a type of chest pain that does not have the typical characteristics of angina. It is usually caused by reduced blood flow to the heart due to narrowing of the coronary arteries, but it does not produce the classic symptoms of angina such as pressure, heaviness, or tightness in the chest.

 

·        Non-anginal chest pain is a type of chest pain that is not caused by reduced blood flow to the heart. It can have various causes, and it is important to determine the underlying cause in order to properly treat it.

 

·        Asymptomatic chest pain refers to chest discomfort or pressure that is present but not accompanied by other symptoms commonly associated with chest pain, such as shortness of breath, dizziness, sweating, or nausea.

 

 

 

RESTECG:

 

Resting electrocardiogram (ECG or EKG) is a non-invasive test that measures the electrical activity of the heart at rest. It is a common diagnostic tool used to evaluate the heart's rhythm and detect any abnormalities or damage to the heart.

 

 

THALACH:

 

Thalach or maximum heart rate is the highest heart rate a person can achieve during exercise. It is an important measure used in assessing cardiovascular fitness and prescribing exercise programs.

 

 

Few other features

 

 

TRESTBPS:

 

It refers to the resting blood pressure of a patient, which is the blood pressure measured while the patient is at rest and not undergoing any physical or mental stress. This feature is often included in cardiovascular disease risk assessment tools and models, as high resting blood pressure is a known risk factor for various cardiovascular conditions, such as heart disease and stroke. The value of TRESTBPS is typically reported in millimetres of mercury (mmHg), with a normal range being around 120/80 mmHg or lower.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Serum Cholesterol:

 

Serum cholesterol refers to the level of cholesterol in the blood. Cholesterol is a waxy, fat-like substance that is essential for the normal functioning of the body, but too much of it can increase the risk of heart disease and stroke.

SVM (SUPPORT VECTOR MACHINE)

Support Vector Machine (SVM) is a supervised machine learning algorithm used for classification and regression analysis. SVM works by finding a hyperplane that separates the data points into different classes in a high-dimensional space.

SVM can be used for both linear and non-linear classification problems. In a linearly separable case, the hyperplane can be represented by a linear equation. However, for non-linearly separable cases, SVM uses a kernel function to transform the data into a higher-dimensional space where it becomes linearly separable.

 

Hyperparameter

SVM hyperparameters are settings that can be adjusted to control the behaviour of the SVM algorithm during training. Tuning these hyperparameters can help to improve the performance of the SVM model.

Here are some common SVM hyperparameters and their descriptions:

·        C: C is the regularization parameter that controls the trade-off between maximizing the margin and minimizing the classification error.

 

·        Kernel type: SVM can use different kernel functions to transform the data into a higher-dimensional space where it can be linearly separated.

 

·        Gamma: Gamma is a kernel parameter that controls the shape of the decision boundary. A smaller gamma value results in a smoother decision boundary, while a larger gamma value results in a more complex and wiggly decision boundary.

 

·        Degree: Degree is a kernel parameter used in polynomial kernels to control the degree of the polynomial function.

 

·        Class weights: SVM allows the assignment of different weights to different classes to balance the class distribution in the training data.

 

·        Max iterations: Max iterations is the maximum number of iterations allowed during the optimization process. Increasing the max iterations may lead to a better solution but may also increase the computational cost.

 

It is important to tune the hyperparameters carefully to avoid overfitting or underfitting.

RANDOM FOREST

Random Forest is a popular supervised machine learning algorithm used for classification, regression, and feature selection. It is an ensemble learning method that combines multiple decision trees to produce a more accurate and stable prediction.
 
How Random Forest works?
 
·        Randomly select a subset of the training data.
 
·        Build a decision tree on the subset of data by selecting the best split point among a random subset of features.
 
·        Repeat steps 1 and 2 to create multiple decision trees.
 
·        For classification, the final prediction is made by majority voting among the predictions of all the trees. For regression, the final prediction is the average of the predictions of all the trees.
 
 
Hyperparameter
 
Random Forest hyperparameters include the number of trees, the maximum depth of the trees, the minimum number of samples required to split a node, the minimum number of samples required to be at a leaf node, and the number of features to consider when looking for the best split point. Hyperparameter tuning can be done using techniques such as grid search, random search, and Bayesian optimization to improve the performance of the Random Forest model.
 

Random Forest has several hyperparameters that can be tuned to optimize the performance of the model. Here are some common hyperparameters and their descriptions:

·        n_estimator:: This hyperparameter determines the number of decision trees to be used in the Random Forest. A higher number of trees can lead to better performance but may also increase the computational cost.
 
·        max_depth: This hyperparameter limits the depth of each decision tree in the Random Forest. A deeper tree can capture more complex relationships in the data, but may also lead to overfitting. Setting a maximum depth can help prevent overfitting.
 
·        min_samples_split: This hyperparameter determines the minimum number of samples required to split an internal node of a decision tree. A higher value can prevent the model from splitting nodes with very few samples, which can help prevent overfitting.
 
·        min_samples_leaf: This hyperparameter determines the minimum number of samples required to be at a leaf node of a decision tree. A higher value can prevent the model from creating leaves with very few samples, which can help prevent overfitting.
 
·        max_features: This hyperparameter determines the maximum number of features to consider when looking for the best split point in each decision tree. 
 
·        bootstrap: Bootstrapping involves randomly sampling the training data with replacement to create different subsets of data for each decision tree.

DECISION TREE

A decision tree is a machine learning algorithm that is commonly used for classification and regression analysis. It is a tree-like model where each internal node represents a test on an attribute, each branch represents the outcome of the test, and each leaf node represents a class label or a numerical value.

The decision tree algorithm works by recursively partitioning the data into smaller subsets based on the most informative attribute at each node. The goal is to create a tree that is as small as possible while accurately classifying or predicting the target variable.

Hyperparameter

Hyperparameters are parameters that are not learned from the data but are set prior to training the model. Hyperparameters in decision trees control the behaviour of the algorithm, such as the depth of the tree, the minimum number of samples required to split a node, the splitting criterion, and the maximum number of features to consider for each split.

Decision has several hyperparameters that can be tuned to optimize the performance of the model. Here are some common hyperparameters and their descriptions:

·        Maximum depth: This hyperparameter limits the depth of the tree, which can prevent overfitting.

 

·        Minimum samples to split: This hyperparameter sets the minimum number of samples required to split an internal node, which can also prevent overfitting.

 

 

·        Splitting criterion: Decision trees can use different criteria for splitting nodes, such as Gini impurity or entropy. This hyperparameter determines the criterion to use.

 

·        Maximum features: This hyperparameter sets the maximum number of features to consider when looking for the best split at each node.

 

 

·        Minimum samples per leaf: This hyperparameter sets the minimum number of samples required to be at a leaf node.

SCORING METHODS

F1 score: In the F1 score, we compute the average of precision and recall. They are both rates, which makes it a logical choice to use the harmonic mean. The F1 score formula is shown here:

 

Precision score: Within everything that has been predicted as a positive, precision counts the percentage that is correct.

·        A less accurate model may find a large number of positives, but because of its noisy selection process, it also falsely finds a large number of positives that aren't positive.

·        A exact model is very "pure": even if it misses some positives, the ones it does classify as positive are almost certainly true.

           

Recall score: Within everything that actually is positive, how many did the model succeed to find

·        Even though they may occasionally misidentify some negative cases as positive, a model with high recall does a good job of finding all the positive instances in the data.

·        Any (or a significant portion of any) positive cases in the data cannot be found by a model with poor recall.

             

 

 

 

Conclusion

Three ML classification modelling techniques have been used to create a model for the detection of cardiovascular disease. By extracting the patient medical history that results in a fatal heart illness from a dataset that contains patients' medical history such as chest pain, sugar level, blood pressure, etc., this method predicts persons with cardiovascular disease. Based on clinical information about a patient's prior heart disease diagnosis, this heart disease detection system helps the patient. The above model was created using the following algorithms KNN , RANDOM FOREST, NAIVE BAYES  LOGISTIC REGRESSION , DECISION TREE and SVM.

Our model has an best accuracy rate of 90.16 % by Random Forest. The likelihood that the model will correctly identify whether a given person has a cardiac condition or not increases with the use of more training data. These computer-assisted tools allow us to anticipate the patient quickly, more accurately, and at a significantly lower cost. We can work with a variety of medical databases since machine learning techniques are more advanced and can anticipate outcomes better than humans, benefiting both patients and medical professionals.

As a result, our research aids in the prediction of patients who are diagnosed with heart problems by cleaning the dataset and using various Ml model to get best  accuracy of 90.16% on our model.
